Staff Here

Oriental DreamWorks is a team of creative, passionate, energetic people who have all come to be a part of building a China-based family branded entertainment company.  Hailing from countries such as China, India, Canada, and the United States, they are based in Shanghai.

LEI LIU

From way, way up in northern China, Lei grew up near Harbin and spent her time trying to stay warm and dreaming of moving to a warmer climate.  She came to Shanghai and attended Shanghai University where she majored in Law.  She then decided to pursue her love of all things legal as well as a hidden passion for American country music and BBQ and headed to law school at Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tennessee.  Finally unfrozen, Lei worked in Houston, Texas before returning to China.  Today she serves as Oriental DreamWorks’ legal dragon warrior and has been a key team member for creating the company as well as keeping it on track for amazing success.

Favorite Movie:
The Shawshank Redeption

Favorite Character from “Kung Fu Panda”:
Mr. Ping (Po’s Duck Dad)

Best Thing about Working in Shanghai:
Work/life balance – so much to do for each!

Best Thing about Working at Oriental DreamWorks:
The opportunity to enlighten your life and broaden your view.

JACK CHEN

Born and raised in Shanghai, Jack knew from a very young age that CG animation was going to be his true love.  After attending Donghua University, he worked on many projects including TV commercials and for a location animation company in Shanghai.  Along the way, he met the founders of 37 Entertainment and joined up.  Leading the charge there, Jack supervised efforts for the “Dragons: Riders of Berk” animated TV series that was created for DreamWorks Animation and based on the “How to Train Your Dragon” franchise.  The show is a big success both in the U.S. on Cartoon Network as well as around the world.  Today, Jack is a CG supervisor for Oriental DreamWorks.

Favorite Movie:
anything by Christopher Nolan, especially “Inception”

Favorite Character from “Kung Fu Panda”:
Po!

Best Thing about Working in Shanghai:
Everything works for me as I never leave!

Best Thing about Working at Oriental DreamWorks:
The work environment. I get to work on feature films and very prestigious projects.

PORTIA XIN

Originally from Beijing, Portia came to Shanghai to attend Tongji University.  She completed her first year before winning a scholarship to both Columbia University and Georgetown University.  She spent two years in New York before returning to Shanghai to graduate with her class.  In between working with top international film festivals and for Shanghai Film Group, she dreamed of getting into film – for real.  After a chance introduction, Portia worked for famed Chinese director, Lu Chuan, doing everything from pre-production to post production to distribution.  In the summer of 2013 while attending the Shanghai Film Festival, Portia ran into a friend who was discussing this new company called Oriental DreamWorks.  Enough said.  She is now part of the production team as a production supervisor. 

Favorite movie:
Shakespeare in Love

Favorite character from Kung Fu Panda: 
Mr. Ping

Best things about working in Shanghai:
I have a life in Shanghai.

Best thing about Oriental DreamWorks:
Your voice will be heard and will be appreciated.
